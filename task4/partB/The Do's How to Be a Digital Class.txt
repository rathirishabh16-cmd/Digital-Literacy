The Do's: How to Be a Digital Class Act
Do Fact-Check Before Sharing: Misinformation spreads faster than truth. Take ten seconds to verify a "breaking news" story or a medical tip before hitting share. Your credibility is your currency.

Do Give Credit Where It’s Due: If you’re posting someone else’s photo, quote, or artwork, tag them. Digital theft is a quick way to get muted—or blocked.

Do Engage Authentically: Social media is meant to be social. Reply to comments, ask questions, and show genuine interest in others' content rather than just "doom-scrolling" or broadcasting.

Do Be Mindful of Privacy: This applies to you and others. Always ask for permission before posting a photo of a friend, and be cautious about sharing real-time locations that could compromise your safety.

Do Practice the "Front Page" Rule: Before you post, ask yourself: "Would I be okay with this appearing on the front page of a newspaper or seen by my boss?" If the answer is no, keep it in the drafts.

The Don’ts: How to Avoid the "Unfollow"
Don’t "Vague-book" or Sub-tweet: Posting cryptic statuses like "I guess I know who my real friends are..." is generally seen as attention-seeking and passive-aggressive. If you have a problem with someone, handle it in a DM or offline.

Don’t Over-Post (Quality over Quantity): Flooding the feed with 15 photos of your lunch or every thought that crosses your mind leads to "content fatigue." Leave some mystery for the imagination.

Don’t Feed the Trolls: Getting into heated, circular arguments with strangers in the comments section rarely ends well. It drains your energy and usually makes everyone involved look bad.

Don’t Use DM's for Unsolicited Pitches: Whether it’s a business opportunity or a "hey girl" multi-level marketing pitch, jumping straight into a sales script without building a rapport is the quickest way to get deleted.

Don’t Forget the Human Behind the Screen: It’s easy to be brave behind a keyboard. If you wouldn't say it to someone’s face in a crowded room, don’t type it in their comments.
